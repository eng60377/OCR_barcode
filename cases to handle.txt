Malaysian passports do not have '<<" to separate surname.
In Malaysian passport, father's name is NOT put in front.
However in boarding pass of Malaysian, father's name IS put in front.

Cases to handle:

1. Boarding pass barcode only up to 22 char for name. If name too long will look like the following:

	E.g.M1Ahmad Badruddin/NorhEEEUG4N
2. Bin, Binti, Binte do not appear in boarding passes, but passports will have these terms. Solved
3. Boarding pass names are sometimes encoded in all caps e.g. ASFIYA, sometimes mixed e.g Ahmad Azhar Ab Hamid. Solved
